William H.
'Bill'
Gates III

Technologist,business leader,philantrophist,world's richest man and all-round nice guy

Early Life

  • 1955-Bill Gates was born and named William Henry "Bill" Gates III in Seattle, Washington state, USA.
  • 1967-Enrolled at the Lakeside school and used his first computer.
  • 1968-Began programming on a computer with Paul Allen at the Lakeside school computer centre.
  • 1969-Wrote Programs for Computer Center Corporation, Information Systems Inc. and Lakeside school.
  • 1972-Started a venture with Paul Allen called Traf-O-Data to read traffic counters.
  • 1973-Graduated from the Lakeside school and enrolled at Harvard University.

Microsoft

  • 1975-Started Micro-Soft with Paul Allen.
  • 1976-Changed the name to Microsoft and registered the company in Albuquerque, New Mexico.
  • 1985-Windows 1.0 launched.
  • 1995-Windows 95 launched.
  • 2008-Left his daily job at Microsoft.
  • 2014-Stepped down as chairman. Remained on the board and began serving as technology advisor.

Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation

  • 2000-Bill and Melinda officially established the foundation. They also announced the first round of Gates Millennium Scholars, part of a $1 billion effort to help 20,000 young people afford college over the next two decades.
  • 2002-The foundation completed efforts to help install 47,000 computers in 11,000 libraries in all 50 states. Ninety-five percent of libraries have computers with Internet access, up from 27 percent in 1996.
  • 2006-Warren Buffett pledged the bulk of his wealth to the foundation.
  • 2010-Bill and Melinda challenged the global health community to declare this the Decade of Vaccines. They pledged $10 billion over the next 10 years to help research, develop, and deliver vaccines for the world’s poorest countries.
  • 2010-Melinda, Warren Buffett, and Bill launched the Giving Pledge, a commitment by the world’s wealthiest people to dedicate most of their wealth to philanthropy.
  • 2013-Bill helped launch a $5.5 billion effort to eradicate polio by 2018. India was certified polio-free by the World Health Organization, leaving only three countries that have never been free of the disease.
